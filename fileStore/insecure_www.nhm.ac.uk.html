Library and Archives

Search our collections

  • Everything
  • Print
  • Online
Advanced search

Curious about the natural world? Then you're in the right place. The Library and Archives has been developing its unparalleled collection of natural history literature, artwork and information resources since 1881.

Museum scientists and visitors can use the search engine to find specific articles and journals, or simply explore our collection pages to discover the treasures of the Library and Archives.

Service updates

Revised opening times for South Kensington from May 2016
Tuesday to Thursday 10.00-16.00.

Reduced access to botanical Library collections: November 2015 - spring 2016
Access to our botany stores is reduced due to major building works. During this period, please give staff at least 48 working hours’ notice for any requests involving the Library’s botanical collection. The amount of material retrieved per appointment will be limited and it may not be possible for us to fulfil larger requests.

Collection highlights

  • The Darwin collection

    The Library holds the world’s most comprehensive collection of material published by Charles Darwin, plus artwork and illustrations from the HMS Beagle voyage

  • The Museum in the First and Second World Wars

    Discover documents and photographs reflecting the Museum’s activities and the experiences of its staff during the World Wars

  • Women artists

    The Library and Archives hold natural history illustrations and artworks of some of the most accomplished women artists spanning the last four centuries
