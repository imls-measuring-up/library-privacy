Workshops & Events

Jul 13

Get to Know Rare Books and Special Collections - 11:00am

Have you ever been curious about what we do or what we have at Rare Books and Special Collections at UBC Library (RBSC)? Join our weekly open house / hands-on show-and-tell for an introduction to our space and our unique materials and collections! The event is free and open to the general public, as well as the UBC community. No need to RSVP, just drop in to learn what RBSC is all about! Rare Books and Special Collections is located on the 1st floor of the Irving K. Barber Learning Centre, 1961 East Mall, UBC Vancouver campus. For more information, please contact Rare Books and Special Collections at (604) 822-0645 or rare.books@ubc.ca.

Register

Jul 14

Copyright and Ethics in Scholarly Publishing - 10:00am

Have questions about plagiarism and academic integrity? What about “self-plagiarism” and “gift authorship”? This workshop will cover what you need to know to get your work out there ethically while preserving your own rights to it.

Register

Jul 14

Pixelating: A Digital Humanities Mixer II (DHSI Roundtable Discussion) - 12:00pm

Please join us for a roundtable discussion about the Digital Humanities Summer Institute. This yearly event at the University of Victoria draws participants from all over North America and aims to provide a collegial environment for learning about computing technologies and how they intersect with different disciplines. In the past, UBC Library staff and UBC faculty and graduate students have attended courses on TEI text encoding, project management, data visualization, programming fundamentals, digitization, and more.

We would like to hear from people who have attended DHSI in the past, to hear your thoughts and experiences on the courses you took and if/how you were able to apply what you learned. As we look to build a community of practice around digital humanities - at the Library and beyond - we are looking for ideas and ways for utilizing our newfound knowledge.

Requirements: None! If you have been to DHSI before we would love to hear your thoughts and experiences. If you haven’t but are interested in DHSI or digital humanities, please join us to learn about it.
